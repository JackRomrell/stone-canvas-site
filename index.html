Why It Matters

Durable Floors Start With the Concrete Underneath.

The finished floor depends on what happens before the coating, polish, stain, or sealer goes down. Grinding, patching, crack repair, joint filling, surface profile, moisture considerations, and product selection all affect the final result.

Proper surface preparation helps prevent peeling and coating failure before it starts.

Crack and joint repair can improve both the finished look and long-term performance of the floor.

Moisture and slab condition affect what systems are appropriate — not every floor is the same.

Traffic, chemicals, cleaning methods, and exposure all factor into which system will hold up over time.

The goal is a floor system that fits how the space is actually used — not a generic off-the-shelf install.
